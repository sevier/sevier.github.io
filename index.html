Isaac Sevier thinks about Energy & Inequality.

I’m an energy engineer, climate justice activist, strategy consultant, and Regional Director for Energy Efficiency for All.

Universal access to housing, water, and clean energy is a basic human right in the 21st century and this principle guides my work.

Having grown up in Tulsa, Oklahoma, worked in the oilfields of Bakersfield, California, and traveled around the world, I am dedicated to fighting for justice and dismantling racist systems as the only path to a democratic future for us all.
